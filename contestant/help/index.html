Different Kinds of Contests

  • help/formal_contest: (TO BE REVISED) About formal contests, in which you have to participate at a designated time, and time is a factor in scoring the contest. You are given problems only on paper, at the start of the contest.
  • help/informal_contest: (TO BE REVISED) About informal contests, in which you may participate on your own time schedule, but in which time is a factor in scoring the contest. There is an operation to get either get all the problems, or to get problems individually, and the time between between when you get a problem and when you submit its solution is used in scoring. Problem statements may be delivered electronically when you get a problem. Scoring is done by an autojudge.
  • help/untimed_contest: (TO BE REVISED) About untimed contests, in which you have as much time as you like to do a problem, without the time affecting your score. Problem statements are put on the web, and scoring is done by an autojudge.

Different Kinds of Accounts

  • Contest Provided Accounts

    You are provided an account by the managers of the contest. For formal contests the managers log in for you. For other contests, you are given an account name and password, and log in remotely using ssh.

    • help/solving: (TO BE REVISED) How to write a solution file, how to test it, how to submit it, when you are using an account given you.
    • help/xterminals: (TO BE REVISED) How to make windows if you are using an xterminal emulator.
    • help/print: (TO BE REVISED) How to print files.

  • Personal Accounts

    You use your own personal account to solve problems, and email submissions and requests to the autojudge.

    • help/email_solving: (TO BE REVISED) How to write a solution file, how to test it, how to submit it, when you are using your own personal account.
